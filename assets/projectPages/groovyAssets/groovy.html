Groovy Groceries

UX Design & Research | Aug. 2021-Aug. 2022

Groovy Groceries is a university grocery app that helps college students find ways to use their school dining dollars, budget their spendings, plan out meals, and grocery shop with ease.

My Role: UX Designer

User research, affinity diagramming, empathy map, user persona, user flow, info architecture, low-high fidelity wireframing, and prototyping

Tools:

Figma and Miro

Team:

Emily Song

Design Question

How might we help student stay on top of their grocery needs?

Research

I conduct user surveys to answer the following key areas of interest:

  1. What are notable preferences and barriers that can impact a student's grocery, meal planning, and budgeting experience?
  2. Understand users' shopping, meal planning, and budgeting processes.

I included both qualitative (free-response) and quantitative (multiple choice) questions within our survey to see how each user differs in their approach to buying groceries, budgeting, and meal planning.

View Survey Responses

Intended respondents: University Students
Survey respondents: Individuals from diverse background and age-groups

Method Limitations:

  • 11 respondents is not a large sample size.
  • Due to sending out the survey to online forums with diverse users, we got a lot of variation in responses and not a lot of responses specifically from university students.

Even though there are limitations with our method of choice, there were still valuable data gathered from the responses. So, in order to observe patterns more succinctly, I created an Affinity Diagram to organize and consolidate commonalities and differences between responses.
