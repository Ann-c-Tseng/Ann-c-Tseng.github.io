Groovy Groceries

UX Design & Research | Aug-Oct 2021, Apr-Aug 2022 (revisit and finalize)

Groovy Groceries is a university grocery app that helps college students find ways to use their school dining dollars, budget their spendings, plan out meals, and grocery shop with ease.

My Role: UX Designer

User research, affinity diagramming, empathy map, user persona, user flow, info architecture, low-high fidelity wireframing, and prototyping

Tools:

Figma and Miro

Team:

Emily Song (UX Designer)

Design Question

How might we help student stay on top of their grocery needs?

Survey Research

I conducted user surveys to find out:

  1. 1. references and barriers that can impact a user's grocery shopping experience.
  2. 2. How a user may go about meal planning and budgeting.

6 Findings

After gathering the responses, I made an affinity diagram to help me consolidate the information.
From there I grouped the common patterns into 6 main findings:

Home Cook?

82% cook at home at least once a weeks

Lists?

Make a physical or mental list before going out

Meal Plan?

Spontaneous, considers busyness & items at home

Priorities?

Store proximity, produce quality, and within budget

Frustrations?

Missing products, lines, and physical setbacks

Budgeting?

Many don't regularly budget or have a set method

Empathy Map

Our users' needs, wants, actions, and thought processes

User Persona

I specified our users' various aspiration, wishes, needs, and struggles

Information Architecture

I then made an information architecture to map out user navigation from one experience flow to another.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

First Round of Mid to High-fidlity Iterations

During this round of iterative design, the designs lacked visual cohesion, appeal, and sufficient user feedback.
I was unsatisfied with them, and so I took some time to study more on User-Experience Interface design, and gain critique on
the designs from UX industry mentors.

Second Round of Iterations

After taking some time for study and rethinking the design -
I came back to reiterate on three main sections: shopping, meal planning, and budgeting.

What I Learned

Grocery purchases, planning, and budgeting can vary diversely between our users. I aimed to create a solution to allow our users the flexibility and efficiency of addressing their particular need, whether that be all experiences this product has to offer or portions of it. Through iterating on specific flows of our solution multiple times, this greatly allowed me to further think through our users' experiences within that particular aspect of our overall solution.

Future Improvements

In the future I would want to achieve a majority of our respondents to be university students within our research, and to incorporate more user interviews within a design cycle. Through doing so, we could gather more feedback on our design and iterate further on those reviews.
