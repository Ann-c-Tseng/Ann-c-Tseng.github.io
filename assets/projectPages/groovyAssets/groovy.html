Groovy Groceries

UX Design & Research | Aug. 2021-Aug. 2022

Groovy Groceries is a university grocery app that helps college students find ways to use their school dining dollars, budget their spendings, plan out meals, and grocery shop with ease.

My Role: UX Designer

User research, affinity diagramming, empathy map, user persona, user flow, info architecture, low-high fidelity wireframing, and prototyping

Tools:

Figma and Miro

Team:

Emily Song

Design Question

How might we help student stay on top of their grocery needs?

Research

I conduct user surveys to answer the following key areas of interest:

  1. What are notable preferences and barriers that can impact a student's grocery, meal planning, and budgeting experience?
  2. Understand users' shopping, meal planning, and budgeting processes.

I included both qualitative (free-response) and quantitative (multiple choice) questions within our survey to see how each user differs in their approach to buying groceries, budgeting, and meal planning.

View Survey Responses

Intended respondents: University Students
Survey respondents: Individuals from diverse background and age-groups

Method Limitations:

  • 11 respondents is not a large sample size.
  • Due to sending out the survey to online forums with diverse users, we got a lot of variation in responses and not a lot of responses specifically from university students.

Even though there are limitations with our method of choice, there were still valuable data gathered from the responses. So, in order to observe patterns more succinctly, I created an Affinity Diagram to organize and consolidate commonalities and differences between responses.

6 Main Findings

Using the Affinity Diagram, I grouped common patterns into a few main findings:

82% of individuals cook at home, at least once a week
Shoppers make a physical or mental list of what they need before going out
Meal planning is often spontaneously done, depending on busyness and the groceries available at home
Shoppers stated grocery store proximity, quality of produce, and financially within budget as their top shopping priorities
Top frustrations with grocery shopping include missing products, checkout lines, and physical setbacks such as inability to carry certain produce
Many shoppers do not have a set method for budgeting on a regular basis

Empathy Map

From the survey findings, I created an empathy map to categorize the data into a more comprehensive diagram that can be used to represent our users' needs, wants, actions, and thought processes.

User Persona

Informed by the empathy map, I then made a user persona to gain a better understanding of our product's user, who they are, what they want and need, and painpoints they encounter.

Information Architecture

I then created an information architecture of our product's layout in order to map out how users will be navigating from one experience flow to another.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

Using the information architecture, I created low-fidelity wireframes to translate our user experience flows into our product's beginning visual concepts, and to further develop our early design decisions.

Initial Iteration High-fidelity Wireframes

From the low-fidelity wireframes, I created the first iteration of high-fidelity wireframes for the product's main screens.

Initial High-fidelity Key Features

I then created prototype flows of Groovy Groceries' key features to more indepthly explore the experiences users can have within the product.

Onboarding

Students can create their Groovy Groceries account, get connected to their school system, and input their adjustible budgeting goal.

Grocery Shopping

The shopping flow leads students to look through stores where they can purchase items from using their student card or credit card. As per student need, there are in-store pickup or delivery options.

Budgeting

Students can look up their weekly balance and set a weekly spending goal to helps them keep track of their weekly spendings. Information on their last transaction, weekly spending, and spending categories are also available.

Meal Planning

Students can set up and view weekly meal plan info such as date, meal type (breakfast, lunch, dinner), name, and any planning notes.

Order History

Students can see a history of their past transactions as well as current delivery purchases. The app will show pending or completed orders & track delivery/pickup status for all orders.

Student Profile

Students can view their student class, major, phone number, and student ID. Additionally, students can log out of the app as needed.
